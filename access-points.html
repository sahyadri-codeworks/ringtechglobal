Wireless Access Points

Access Points (APs) enable wireless connectivity by extending wired networks into WiFi networks, allowing devices such as laptops, smartphones, and IoT devices to connect seamlessly.

Modern APs support advanced standards like WiFi 6 and WiFi 7, delivering higher speeds, improved efficiency, and better performance in high-density environments. Features such as band steering, MU-MIMO, roaming, and centralized cloud management enhance user experience and network control.

They are widely deployed in offices, campuses, hotels, retail spaces, and public venues, providing reliable and scalable wireless connectivity.
